Visualizing the landscape of neural networks

Training neural networks requires minimizing a loss function. What does this function look like?

We know it is high-dimensional, most likely non-convex, and visualizing it is non-trivial. Recently, I came across an intriguing paper, Visualizing the loss landscape of neural nets by Hao Li and others that proposes a simple visualization technique for plotting the loss function. What I like about this technique, without going into much detail, is that it proposes a way to normalize the visualizations across various networks so that they are comparable.

With the help of TensorFlow.js, we'll use this technique to explore how initialization, network architecture and hyper parameters affect the loss landscape. All of the simulations run in the browser, and use the MNIST dataset.
